Pizza Margherita is a classic Italian pizza known for its simple yet delicious combination of fresh ingredients. It features a thin, crispy crust topped with a flavorful tomato sauce, fresh mozzarella cheese, and basil leaves. Here's how to make a traditional Pizza Margherita:

⋆˚ʚɞ Ingredients ⋆˚ʚɞ

For the Dough:

  • 2 1/4 tsp (7g) active dry yeast
  • 1 1/2 cups (360ml) warm water (110°F or 45°C)
  • 4 cups (500g) all-purpose flour
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1 tsp sugar (optional, helps activate the yeast)

For the Tomato Sauce:

  • 1 can (15 oz) crushed tomatoes
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • 1 tsp dried basil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

For the Toppings:

  • 8 oz (225g) fresh mozzarella cheese, sliced or torn into pieces
  • Fresh basil leaves
  • Extra virgin olive oil (for drizzling)
  • Freshly ground black pepper (optional)

⋆˚ʚɞ Instructions ⋆˚ʚɞ

  1. Prepare the Dough:
    • In a small bowl, dissolve the yeast (and sugar, if using) in warm water. Let it sit for about 5-10 minutes, until it becomes frothy.
    • In a large mixing bowl or stand mixer, combine the flour and salt. Make a well in the center and add the yeast mixture and olive oil.
    • Mix until a dough forms. Knead the dough on a floured surface or using a stand mixer with a dough hook for about 7-10 minutes, until smooth and elastic.
    • Place the dough in a lightly oiled bowl, cover it with a damp cloth or plastic wrap, and let it rise in a warm place for 1-2 hours, or until it has doubled in size.
  2. Prepare the Tomato Sauce:
    • In a medium saucepan, heat olive oil over medium heat.
    • Add minced garlic and cook for about 1 minute until fragrant.
    • Stir in the crushed tomatoes, oregano, basil, salt, and pepper. Simmer for 10-15 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the sauce thickens. Adjust seasoning as needed.
  3. Assemble the Pizza:
    • Preheat your oven to its highest setting (typically 475-500°F or 245-260°C). If using a pizza stone, place it in the oven to preheat as well.
    • Punch down the risen dough and divide it into 2 or 3 equal portions, depending on the size of the pizzas you want to make.
    • Roll out one portion of dough on a floured surface into a thin circle or rectangle. Transfer the rolled dough to a parchment-lined baking sheet or a pizza peel if using a pizza stone.
    • Spread a thin layer of tomato sauce over the dough, leaving a small border around the edges.
    • Evenly distribute slices or pieces of fresh mozzarella over the sauce.
  4. Bake the Pizza:
    • Bake the pizza in the preheated oven for 10-15 minutes, or until the crust is golden and crispy and the cheese is melted and bubbly.
    • Remove the pizza from the oven and immediately top with fresh basil leaves. Drizzle with a little extra virgin olive oil and sprinkle with freshly ground black pepper if desired.
  5. Serve:
    • Let the pizza cool for a few minutes before slicing and serving. Enjoy it fresh from the oven!

⋆˚ʚɞ Tips ⋆˚ʚɞ

  • Pizza Stone: Using a preheated pizza stone can help achieve a crispy crust.
  • Dough: If you have time, letting the dough rise slowly in the refrigerator overnight can develop more flavor.
  • Mozzarella: Fresh mozzarella is key for the authentic taste and texture. Avoid using pre-grated cheese if possible.
